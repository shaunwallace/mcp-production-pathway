# [Phase title or memo subject]

**Audience:** [who this is written for — exec, self, future interviewer]  
**Length target:** 1 page (600-800 words)  
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD

## TL;DR

Three bullet points. No more. If it takes four, the memo isn't tight enough yet.

- Point one
- Point two
- Point three

## Context

Two paragraphs maximum. Why this memo exists, who should care, what decision it informs.

## Argument

Prose. Opinionated. Anti-jargon. Use specific examples from your own work. If you find yourself writing "leveraging" or "synergies," stop and restart the paragraph.

Make one claim per paragraph. Support it with evidence. Name the trade-off.

Structure that works: start with the strongest claim, defend it, concede what the opposing view gets right, land on your recommendation.

## What I'd say in a design review

Two or three sentences. The version you'd actually say out loud, not the version dressed up for writing. This is the bit that people will quote.

## What I changed my mind on

One thing. If nothing, say so and explain why your priors were right. This is the section that signals intellectual honesty, which is what makes the memo credible.
